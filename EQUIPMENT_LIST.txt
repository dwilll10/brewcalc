RECOMMENDED RETAILERS
======================

Online Homebrew Shops:
  - Northern Brewer         northernbrewer.com        Wide selection, good kits, fast shipping
  - MoreBeer                morebeer.com              Great prices, bulk ingredients, free shipping over $59
  - Adventures in Homebrewing  homebrewing.org        Competitive pricing, good sales
  - Midwest Supplies        midwestsupplies.com       Beginner-friendly, owned by Northern Brewer

Electronics / Raspberry Pi:
  - Adafruit                adafruit.com              Best tutorials and documentation for Pi projects
  - SparkFun                sparkfun.com              Good sensor/relay selection, learning resources
  - PiShop.us               pishop.us                 Pi-specific retailer, usually in stock
  - Micro Center            microcenter.com           Great in-store prices on Pi and SD cards

General / Amazon Alternatives:
  - Amazon                  amazon.com                Fastest shipping, widest selection, compare prices
  - Walmart                 walmart.com               OxiClean, scales, basic kitchen items
  - Home Depot / Lowe's     homedepot.com             Styrofoam coolers, insulation, project boxes

Local Homebrew Shop (LHBS):
  - Find yours at homebrewersassociation.org/find-a-homebrew-supply-shop
  - Benefits: hands-on advice, can inspect equipment, often have fresh yeast
    and bulk grains, and support local business


NOTES
======

1. Start with bottling for serving — it's cheaper and simpler. Upgrade to the
   mini keg after your first few successful batches.

2. The wort chiller is optional at 1.75 gallons. An ice bath (sink full of ice
   water) cools the small volume in 15-20 minutes. A chiller is faster and
   worth it if you plan to scale up to 5-gallon batches.

3. Save pry-off commercial beer bottles (NOT twist-off) — rinse them after
   drinking and you get free bottles. Brown glass is best for UV protection.

4. The Raspberry Pi setup can wait until your second or third brew. Use a
   simple stick-on temperature strip and manual monitoring for your first batch
   to learn the process before automating.

5. Star San is a no-rinse sanitizer — mix 1 oz per 5 gallons of water. Keep a
   spray bottle of it handy. It lasts months in a sealed container. If in doubt,
   sanitize it.

6. Buy ingredients per recipe rather than stocking up. Liquid malt extract and
   yeast have limited shelf life. Hops should be stored in the freezer.
